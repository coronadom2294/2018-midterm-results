Rarely has Minnesota seen an election season with so many hotly-contested races on both a state and national level, including many that are open or had surprise primary results. On a national level, our unprecedented number of toss-up congressional races — among the most in the nation — could help decide which party controls the House and Senate. Enthusiasm has been high, and so have early vote counts, which are pacing with 2016 despite usual trends for midterm years to see lower voter participation.

Here you'll find results for top state executive offices, counts of where congressional party power stands nationally, and Minnesota's 10 races for U.S. House and Senate.

U.S. Congress: Balance of Power
